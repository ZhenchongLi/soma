## Real issues

- `soma_delegate:status_task/3` inserts every forwarded caller into `status_requests` at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate.erl:74-84` and removes it only after a coordinator reply or `DOWN`. It never expires the entry or observes the caller. I suspended one coordinator and issued 25 status calls with 20 ms caller timeouts. All 25 callers exited. The permanent ingress still retained all 25 pid/ref records. A slow coordinator turns status polling into unbounded ingress state. This breaks the stated routing-only ownership boundary.
- Cancellation has no forced-stop backstop. `arm_forced_stop_timer/6` returns `undefined` for `cancelled` at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:485-495`, and the forced-stop handler at lines 154-171 accepts only `cancel_status := timeout`. I suspended a live LLM-phase round worker and called `soma_delegate:cancel/1`. After 1.5 seconds the worker and LLM child were alive, the ingress retained the cancel waiter, and no reply arrived. An unresponsive worker makes cancellation hang forever. Arm the backstop for cancellation and preserve `in_doubt` when the killed worker had dispatched unsafe work.
- `valid_round_result/1` at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:514-519` checks only encoded size and status. It accepts pids, refs, authentication state, product conversation data, and raw-lease-shaped terms in checkpoint and ledger deltas. `commit_round_deltas/2` stores them, then `round_snapshot/1` copies them into the next worker. I sent one identity-matching result with those values and round 2 received every forbidden value, including the pid and ref. Validate the allowed result schema and safe term types before commit. The current size check does not enforce the task-only snapshot boundary.
