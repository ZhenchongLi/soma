## Real issues

- `soma_delegate:status_task/2` calls a coordinator synchronously from the registered ingress at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate.erl:61`. A coordinator exit propagates through that call and kills the ingress before its queued `DOWN` can be handled. I suspended a coordinator, started `status/1`, then killed the coordinator. The ingress restarted, lost every route, and a duplicate `request_id` minted `delegate-task-4` instead of returning `delegate-task-1`. This breaks crash isolation, status routing, and request deduplication. A child exit must become task data without taking down the route owner.
- Terminal projections are not bounded. `round_projection/2` copies an arbitrary round reason at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:490`, and `store_terminal_projection/4` retains it unchanged at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate.erl:171`. A normal `fail` tool with an 8,000-byte reason produced an 8,044-byte terminal projection and an 8,153-byte public status reply. The promised 512-byte projection cap is absent, and raw secrets or process-local terms can survive coordinator cleanup in the ingress.
- Cancellation is not terminal once the coordinator accepts it. `commit_finished_round/4` ignores `cancel_status => cancelled` at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:270`, so a matching success result racing with cancellation wins. I paused the worker, waited until the coordinator stored `cancel_status => cancelled`, then delivered a valid matching success message. `soma_delegate:cancel/1` returned `status => succeeded`. The `handle_round_worker_down/3` branch at line 350 also classifies a cancelled unsafe worker loss as `cancelled` before checking the unsafe marker, hiding an unknown state-tool outcome. Cancellation must win known pre-stateful races; a lost unsafe result must remain `in_doubt`.
- The valid-result path violates the required commit order. `commit_round_result/5` emits `delegate.round.completed`, cancels timers, drops the monitor, and terminates the worker at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:248`; it does not apply checkpoint, usage, mutation, unknown-outcome, and terminal deltas until `commit_finished_round/4` at line 270. A blocked or failed synchronous worker removal leaves a durable completion event with no committed state. Commit the validated result before clearing the worker and before starting the next round.
- Delegate events leak arbitrary binary failure reasons. `normalize_reason_class/1` accepts every binary unchanged at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_event.erl:78`. A production delegate action using the built-in `fail` tool with reason `<<"sk-live-production-secret">>` copied that secret into `delegate.round.completed`, `delegate.task.cleanup`, and `delegate.task.terminal`. The sentinel-only clauses in `scrub_term/1` do not scrub real secrets. Map failures to a bounded reason-class allowlist before writing public events.
