- Timeouts lose to late success. The action deadline at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_round_worker.erl:189-195` only sends `cancel`; the `run_completed` handler at lines 137-149 ignores the stored timeout. The overall deadline has the same hole for unsafe work: the timeout branch at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:268-279` requires `unsafe_action_dispatched := false`, so a late result from a non-idempotent action falls into the success branch at lines 289-294. A 200 ms round deadline produced `succeeded` after a state action. These owners do not enforce their deadlines.

I used the submitting process pid as a lease name. The round snapshot contained `resource_handles => #{<0.10.0> => <<"opaque">>}`. Process-local state reaches every worker despite the task-only snapshot contract.
- The coordinator does not monitor its lease guard. `acquire_scoped_leases/1` stores only the guard pid at `apps/soma_actor/src/soma_delegate_coordinator.erl:626-645`, and cleanup calls that pid unconditionally at lines 650-655. I killed the guard after the round started. The task remained `running`; cancellation then crashed the coordinator on `release_all` with `noproc`, the ingress published `coordinator_crashed`, and the adapter recorded zero releases. A guard failure leaves stale handles active and can leak the raw lease instead of failing the task at the ownership boundary.
